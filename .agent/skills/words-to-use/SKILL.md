# Words to Use — Approved BDR Vocabulary

These are the specific words and phrases that work in Phrase outreach. They signal technical credibility, peer-to-peer tone, and genuine helpfulness.

## Greeting Words
| Use This | Context |
|----------|---------|
| "Hey" | Always. The only greeting. |
| `{{first_name}}` | Always after "Hey". Never abbreviated. |

## Hook Verbs (Opening Sentence)
| Use This | Instead Of |
|----------|-----------|
| "Noticed" | "I saw that", "I discovered" |
| "Saw" | "I came across", "I stumbled upon" |
| "Spotted" | "I found", "I identified" |

**Rule:** Lead with the verb, not "I". Write "Noticed you added..." not "I noticed you added..."
- Best: "Noticed you added `react-i18next`..."
- Acceptable: "Saw the discussion about i18n..."
- Avoid: "I noticed that your team has been working on..."

## Tone Words (Sound Human)
| Use This | Effect |
|---|---|
| "usually" | Softens claims — "That usually means..." |
| "typically" | Same — "Teams typically spend..." |
| "looks like" | Observation, not assertion — "Looks like active work" |
| "tends to" | Pattern language — "This tends to become a bottleneck" |
| "often" | Frequency without certainty — "Teams often hit complexity here" |
| "might" | Permission language — "This might be relevant" |
| "happy to" | Availability without pressure — "Happy to share" |
| "if useful" | Low pressure qualifier — "Here if useful" |
| "no worries" | Permission to decline — "No worries at all" |
| "here whenever" | Open door — "Here whenever timing is right" |

## CTA Words
| Word/Phrase | Energy Level |
|---|---|
| "Worth a look?" | Low — ideal for Email 1 |

| "Curious if..." | Medium — frames as mutual discovery |
| "Worth exploring?" | Medium — collaborative |

## Temporal Words (Signal Age)
| Signal Age | Use This |
|---|---|
| < 30 days | "recently" — "recently added" |
| 30-90 days | "earlier this quarter" |
| 90-180 days | "earlier this year" |
| > 180 days | Drop temporal language entirely |
