# CTA Formulas — Call-to-Action Patterns

The CTA is a question that asks for INTEREST, not TIME. You are never asking for a meeting in cold email. You are asking if the topic is relevant.

## The CTA Spectrum (Low → High Energy)

### Energy Level 1: Feather Touch (Breakup / Email 3)
These barely ask for anything. They leave the door open.

| CTA | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "Here if useful." | Email 3 or breakup |
| "Here whenever timing is right." | Breakup email |
| "No pressure — just thought it might be relevant." | Email 3 |
| "Happy to share if useful." | Email 3 value-add |

### Energy Level 2: Soft Ask (Email 1 Default)
These ask for interest without committing to anything.

| CTA | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "Worth a look?" | Email 1, default for all personas |
| "Worth exploring?" | Email 1 or 2, collaborative framing |
| "Curious if this resonates?" | Email 2, seeking validation |
| "Relevant to what you're building?" | Email 1 for technical personas |

### Energy Level 3: Medium Ask (Email 2)
These suggest a next step without specifying what it is.

| CTA | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "Open to seeing how we fit into your CI/CD?" | Email 1-2, VP Engineering persona |
| "Open to a quick look?" | Email 2, after initial value delivery |
| "Open to comparing workflows?" | Email 1-2, Dir Localization persona |
| "Happy to show you what that looks like in practice." | Email 2-3, statement CTA |
| "Worth a quick look at how we speed up international launches?" | Email 1-2, Head of Product persona |

### Energy Level 4: Direct Ask (ONLY After Reply)
These are for REPLIES ONLY. Never use in cold outreach sequences.

| CTA | When to Use |
|---|---|
| "Want me to set up a quick sandbox?" | After positive reply |
| "Happy to walk through a 15-min demo." | After "tell me more" reply |
| "Want to see it in action with your repo?" | After technical interest |

## CTA Selection by Persona

| Persona | Default CTA (Email 1) | Email 2 CTA | Email 3 CTA |
|---|---|---|---|
| VP Engineering | "Open to seeing how we fit into your CI/CD?" | "Worth exploring?" | "Happy to share what that looks like." |
| Head of Product | "Worth a quick look at how we speed up international launches?" | "Curious if this resonates?" | "Here if useful." |
| Dir Localization | "Open to comparing workflows?" | "Worth exploring?" | "Happy to share if useful." |
| Default/Unknown | "Worth a look?" | "Open to a quick look?" | "Here if useful." |

## CTA Selection by Email Position

| Email | Energy Level | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Email 1 | 2 (Soft) | "Worth a look?" |
| Email 2 | 2-3 (Soft-Medium) | "Open to a quick look?" |
| Email 3 | 1 (Feather) | "Here if useful." |
| Email 4 (Breakup) | 1 or Question | "Should I close the loop on this?" |

## CTA Formatting Rules
- Always a question (ends with ?)
- Exception: statement CTAs like "Happy to share..." (Email 3 only)
- Standalone on its own line (not embedded in a paragraph)
- One CTA per email. Never two. Never zero.
- No exclamation marks after CTAs

## CTA Anti-Patterns (BANNED)

| Banned CTA | Why | Better Alternative |
|---|---|---|
| "Can we schedule a call?" | Too much commitment | "Worth a look?" |
| "Do you have 15 minutes?" | Asking for time, not interest | "Open to a quick look?" |
| "Would love to set up a demo" | About your wants, not theirs | "Worth exploring?" |
| "Let me know your thoughts" | Vague, passive | "Curious if this resonates?" |
| "Can I send you more info?" | You already did | State the value, don't ask permission |
| "Would you be the right person?" | Puts them on the spot | Route to them and let them redirect |
| "Feel free to reach out" | All burden on them | "Here if useful." |
| "I'd love to pick your brain" | Ick. Just ick. | Don't write this. |
| "Can we hop on a quick call?" | "Hop on" is cringe | Never say "hop on" |
| "Let's connect!" | LinkedIn energy in email | Ask a specific question |

## The CTA Test
Read your CTA out loud. Does it sound like something you'd text a friend about a restaurant recommendation? ("Worth checking out?" / "Heard of this place?") If yes, it works. If it sounds like something from a sales training manual, rewrite.
