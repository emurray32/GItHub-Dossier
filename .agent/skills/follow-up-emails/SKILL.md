# Follow-Up Emails (Emails 2 & 3)

The prospect didn't reply to Email 1. That's normal — response rates are 3-8%. These emails must earn attention with a NEW angle each time.

## The Cardinal Rule
**NEVER repeat the Email 1 hook.** If Email 1 said "Noticed you added react-i18next," Email 2 cannot say "Following up on my note about react-i18next." They already read it. Or they didn't. Either way, repeating it adds zero value.

## Email 2: The Different Angle

### Purpose
Reframe the value from a completely different direction. If Email 1 was technical, Email 2 is business-impact. If Email 1 was about the specific signal, Email 2 is about the category problem.

### Structure
```
Line 1: "Hey {{first_name}},"
Line 2: [NEW ANGLE — industry pain, stat, or different framing]
Line 3: [PHRASE VALUE — from this new angle]
Line 4: [CTA — still soft, but slightly different ask]
Line 5: "{{sender_first_name}}"
```

### Approved Email 2 Angles

**Angle A: The Time Sink**
"Teams at your stage typically spend 30-40% of i18n engineering time on manual file handoffs. Phrase eliminates that — locale files stay in lockstep with your branches via GitHub Sync."

**Angle B: The Process Pain**
"One thing I keep seeing: i18n starts as a side project, then becomes a bottleneck at launch. Wiring in automation early prevents that scramble."

**Angle C: The Industry Pattern**
"Companies making this same move — adding i18n infrastructure — usually hit a fork: build the translation pipeline in-house or plug in automation. Most wish they'd plugged in earlier."

**Angle D: The Speed Play (for Product personas)**
"International launches usually stall on localization handoffs, not engineering. Phrase keeps translations moving at sprint speed so launches don't slip."

### Email 2 Rules
- Open with a NEW insight, not a reference to Email 1
- Never say "following up", "circling back", "just checking in"
- Never say "as I mentioned" or "in my last email"
- Can reference a general pattern ("teams at your stage") but not a specific Phrase customer by name
- Body: 60-90 words
- End with a different CTA than Email 1

### Email 2 Approved CTAs
- "Open to a quick look?"
- "Happy to show you what that looks like in practice."
- "Worth exploring?"
- "Curious if this resonates?"

## Email 3: The Lighter Touch

### Purpose
Add value. Share an insight. Lower the pressure to zero. This email should feel like a helpful FYI, not a sales follow-up.

### Structure
```
Line 1: "Hey {{first_name}},"
Line 2: [VALUE-ADD — an insight, resource, or observation]
Line 3: [SOFT BRIDGE — optional, connect it back lightly]
Line 4: [ULTRA-SOFT CTA]
Line 5: "{{sender_first_name}}"
```

### Approved Email 3 Approaches

**Approach A: The Insight Share**
"One thing teams overlook early in the i18n build: setting up continuous localization before the first launch saves weeks of catch-up later. Translation debt compounds fast once you ship."

**Approach B: The "Thought of You" Resource**
"Saw an interesting pattern in how teams handle i18n at scale — the ones that automate file sync early avoid the 'translation freeze' problem entirely."

**Approach C: The Observation**
"Quick thought — most teams that add [library] end up needing a translation management layer within a quarter. Happy to share how that typically plays out if useful."

### Email 3 Rules
- Shortest email in the sequence (40-60 words ideal)
- Feel like a human observation, NOT a pitch
- No mention of Phrase features
- Light or no product mention — just the insight
- If mentioning Phrase, keep it to "happy to share how" not "Phrase does X"
- Absolutely no pressure language

### Email 3 Approved CTAs
- "Happy to share what that looks like in practice if useful."
- "No pressure — just thought it might be relevant."
- "Let me know if any of this resonates."
- "Here if it's ever useful."

## Follow-Up Anti-Patterns (NEVER Do These)

| Bad Pattern | Why It Fails | Instead |
|---|---|---|
| "Just following up..." | Adds no value, screams sales email | Open with a new insight |
| "Bumping this to the top of your inbox" | Aggressive, entitled | They saw it; bring something new |
| "Haven't heard back..." | Guilt trip | Don't reference silence |
| "I know you're busy..." | Patronizing | They know they're busy |
| "Did you get my last email?" | Needy, desperate | Assume they did; move on |
| "Quick reminder about..." | You're not their calendar | New angle, new value |
| "As I mentioned previously..." | Repetitive, lazy | Never reference prior emails |
| Repeating the same hook | Shows you have one trick | Each email = fresh angle |

## Spacing Between Emails
- Email 1 → Email 2: 3-4 business days
- Email 2 → Email 3: 4-5 business days
- These are configured in Apollo sequences, not in the email body
